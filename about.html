What happens at SOUP?

Soup is a collabrative and democratic microgranting nonprofit organization. For a suggested donation of 5 dollars, anyone who attends receives a vote, a voice, and a dinner. Each meeting begins with presentations by 4 individuals who each represent an organization of their choice. These individuals have 5 minutes to present, after which they can answer up to 3 audience questions. Once these presenters finish, we invite all attendees to eat, talk, and share resources among themselves and the people they meet. At this time, we also invite anyone attending to vote for whichever charity they think is most deserving of help. At the end of the night, we'll count the votes, and whoever recieced the most earns all of the money from the admittance fees. Winners are asked to return to a later meeting to share their successes explain how the money was used.

Our Mission:

BH SOUP is dedicated to promoting continued development of the community by enabling philanthropy based in democracy, and to providing a fun, creative, and collaberative environment for all people, regardless of age, gender, religion, nationality, or any other characteristic.

Our Values:

BH SOUP values creativity, cooperation, community, democracy, and philanthropy.

Our Goals:

  • Encourage Philanthropy

  • Promote Democratic Principles

  • Enable Community Action

  • Cultivate Inclusivity

  • Foster Collaboration

Our Inspiration:

BH SOUP was inspired by Detroit SOUP, founded and run by Amy Kharel. Our founder was introduced to this nonprofit by Sophia Kapur, founder of I Am Not A Statistic. Both have been invaluable in the formation of this charity, and have our deepest thanks.
